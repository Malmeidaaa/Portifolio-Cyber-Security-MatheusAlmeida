Certifications

CompTIA Security+

CompTIA Security+ is the official gateway for anyone looking to build a solid career in cybersecurity. It is globally recognized as the standard that validates we have the necessary foundation to effectively protect systems and networks. What I find most interesting is that it is 'vendor-neutral,' meaning it doesn’t focus on a specific brand of equipment. The focus is on learning universal security principles: threat analysis, network architecture, cryptography, and incident response. It is knowledge that you can take anywhere. Having this certification on my resume is proof that I don’t just understand the theory, but I also have the technical skills to identify vulnerabilities and mitigate risks in practice. It is a huge advantage that many large companies and even governments require. Basically, it is the first big step for anyone who, like me, wants to become a cyber defense specialist.

Threat Analysis and Mitigation Network Architecture and Security Identity and Access Management (IAM) Incident Response and Basic Digital Forensics Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) Cryptography and PKI Analytical Thinking Effective Communication Ethics and Integrity Adaptability

Google IT Support

Those who earn the Google IT Support Professional Certificate have completed five-courses, developed by Google, that include hands-on, practice-based assessments and are designed to prepare them for entry-level roles in IT support. They are competent in foundational skills, including troubleshooting and customer service, networking, operating systems, system administration, and security.

Operating System Administration Network Security Version Control Package and Software Management Computer Hardware Systems Administration IT Infrastructure TCP/IP Chef (Configuration Management Tool) Network Troubleshooting File Systems

Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate

Those who earn the Google Cybersecurity Certificate, developed by Google, have demonstrated their competence in foundational cybersecurity tactics. Through hands-on activities and assessments, graduates develop proficiency in identifying common risks and vulnerabilities and using Python, Linux, SQL, SIEM tools, and Intrusion Detection Systems to mitigate threats, preparing them for entry-level roles in cybersecurity.

Authentication Cybersecurity Assessment Information Assurance Information Security Information Systems Intrusion Detection Linux Network Security NIST Framework Python SIEM SQL Threat Analysis Vulnerability Assessment

Junior Cybersecurity Analyst CISCO

I completed the Junior Cybersecurity Analyst Career Path, which prepared me for the entry-level Cisco Certified Support Technician (CCST) Cybersecurity certification. This training was the foundation that qualified me for positions such as Cybersecurity Technician, Junior Cybersecurity Analyst, and Tier 1 Help Desk Support roles.

Threat Intelligence Network Security Risk Management CCST Cybersecurity Incident Response Problem Solving Team Collaboration
